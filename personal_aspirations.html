Personal Aspirations

Zero-Waste Goals

Goals that can be achieved are:

Repair Instead of Replace and Wasting

Fix broken items like electronics, clothing, and furniture instead of discarding them.

Reducing Our Use Of Paper To Reduce Paper Waste

Use digital notes, opt for email receipts, and say no to unnecessary printed materials which helps reduce paper waste.

Keep Leftovers In Proper Places Instead of Wasting

Store excess food properly to prevent spoilage and to ensure that food will not be wasted but eaten.

Carry a Reusable Coffee Cup

Bring your own reusable cups to cafes or out to avoid disposable cups and plastic bottles

Challenges of these goals

However, there are many challenges to these goals such as:

Cost of Repairs and Lack of Knowledge of Repair

Sometimes, repairing an item can be more expensive than buying a new one, especially for electronics and not everyone has the skills or tools to fix broken items themselves.

Consumer Awareness and Habitual Usage of Paper

Many individuals are unaware of the environmental impact of excessive paper use and many workplaces and institutions still rely heavily on printed documents.

Storage Space Issues

A messy and cluttered storage space makes it hard to locate leftovers which can lead to the leftovers not being eaten and being wasted

Limited Acceptance

In the Singaporean context, plastic cups are the default use and it will cause incovinience for drink sellers in hawker centres, for example, a hard time.
